General Waker-Uz-Zaman

SBP, OSP, SGP, PSC
Chief of Army Staff

General Waker-Uz-Zaman, SBP, OSP, SGP, psc assumed the role of Chief of Army Staff of the Bangladesh Army on 23 June 2024, bringing over three decades of distinguished military experience. He has held key command, staff, and instructional roles, including Chief of General Staff, Military Secretary, and Principal Staff Officer to the Prime Minister. An alumnus of prestigious institutions like King’s College London and the UK’s Joint Services Command and Staff College, he has contributed significantly to UN peacekeeping, military modernization, and national events. He was honored with the Extraordinary Service Medal and Army Medal of Glory.

Adjutant General, Bangladesh Army

Major General Md Masudur Rahman, ndc, psc recently completed a successful tenure as GOC of 10 Infantry Division and Area Commander in Cox's Bazar. Commissioned in 1991 into the Infantry Corps, he has held key command, staff, and instructional roles, including Defence Attaché in Myanmar and service in UN missions (UNIKOM and UNMIL). A distinguished academic, he holds multiple advanced degrees, including from King's College London and BUP, and has completed strategic and language training in various countries. His career spans assignments and training across over 15 nations.

Brig. Gen. Md. Nishatul Islam Khan

NDC, PSC

Brig Gen Md Nishatul Islam Khan, commissioned in 1993, has held diverse command, staff, and instructional roles across regiments, headquarters, and training institutions, including the National Defence College and DGFI. He commanded an infantry regiment and brigade, served in key positions such as Deputy Commander of the President Guard Regiment, and contributed as Directing Staff for the Armed Forces War Course. A graduate of prestigious institutions like the National Defence College, Mirpur, and UK Defence Academy, he also earned a Master's in Security and Strategic Studies from Tanzania. He participated in UN missions in Sierra Leone and Sudan and visited 32 countries. Currently, he serves as Director, Internal Affairs Bureau at HQ DGFI.

Brig. Gen. Md. Sajjad Hossain

SUP, NDC, AFWC, PSC

Brig Gen Sajjad of the Bangladesh Army, born on 22 November 1972 in Rangamati, was commissioned on 16 December 1993. He holds three master's degrees in national security, strategy and development, and defense studies from prestigious institutions, along with diplomas and certifications in supply chain, operations, HR, project management, and strategic leadership. His diverse career includes commanding regimental units, serving as an instructor at ASC Centre and School and Bangladesh Military Academy, and holding staff roles such as ADC to CAS and Director of Welfare and Rehabilitations. He has also contributed significantly in three UN missions in logistics and training roles.

NDU, PSC

Brig Gen Shams Mohammad Mamun, ndu, psc, born on 15 July 1976 in Chattogram, excelled academically, ranking in the top ten in SSC and HSC exams from Cumilla Cadet College. Brig. Gen. Md. Mohashin Reza

OSP, AFWC, PSC

Brigadier General Md Mohashin Reza, OSP, afwc, psc, commissioned in 1997, has commanded various units in the Bangladesh Army and served in key staff and instructional roles, including at DSCSC, Dhaka. A graduate of DSCSC Bangladesh and the Royal Saudi Arabian Combined Staff College, he also completed the Armed Forces War Course at NDC, Bangladesh, earning a master's in defense studies. Independent Director

Mr. Anisuddin Ahmed Khan

Mr. Anisuddin Ahmed Khan, a Fellow of the Institute of Bankers, Bangladesh, is a seasoned banker and former Managing Director & CEO of Mutual Trust Bank Limited (2009-2019). With over 38 years of experience across leading financial institutions, he specializes in corporate banking, credit operations, risk management, and financial inclusion. He has presented papers at Harvard and Yale, attended leadership training at Cambridge and INSEAD, and served as Chairman of ABB and PDBL. Currently, he is an Adjunct Professor at IUB, Independent Director at Berger Paints, and involved in multiple corporate and philanthropic organizations.

Independent Direcetor

Nusrat Khan

Nusrat Khan, born on 13 March 1983 in Dhaka, is a charismatic and hardworking leader who inspires peers with her dedication and excellence. She began her career as a lecturer at East West University and later joined the Department of Finance at the University of Dhaka, where she is now an Associate Professor. With an MSc in Management of Risks (distinction) from St. John's University, New York, she is a Fulbright Scholar and a member of the Beta Gamma Sigma honor society. Renowned for her academic excellence and numerous journal publications, she also serves as an independent director of Bangladesh Welding Electrodes Ltd. Her achievements have earned her widespread admiration in academia and beyond.
